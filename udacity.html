I love Udacity! (No, they didn't pay me to say that.) I first learned about it when reading about Sebastin Thrun and Google's self driving cars. When I heard that he was starting an online course to teach the programming behind self driving cars, I jumped on it immediately. I entered their first two courses on the day the site launched -- Intro to Computer Science taught by David Evans, and Artificial Intelligence for Robotics taught by Sebastian Thrun himself.

Udacity is pretty popular these days, and it was really fun to be there at the beginning. I hung out in the IRC channel (and actually created the first channel for CS101) and forums, and thoroughly enjoyed both classes. While I already had a solid base in programming, CS101 was still fascinating. David Evans is a fantastic instructor, and it was my first real exposure to Python. (Python is now my favorite language to program in.) I unfortunately had to stop listening to the AI for Robotics lectures about 4 weeks into the class, as I was running out of time between work, school, and taking Udacity classes. I did completely finish CS101 though.

Since then, I've taken all of their web apps course (which was amazing), and portions of their programming languages and design of computer programs classes. I plan on taking more, as soon as I can devote the time to it.

I've tried both Khan Academy and Coursera as well, but must admit that I definitely prefer Udacity's format and course quality.

I've got the source for a few of the projects I did at Udacity up on GitHub. Be warned, these were class projects and I haven't touched most of them since the class finished, so the quality may vary.

  • Basic Search Engine, for CS101
  • Basic Blog Software, for the Web Apps course
  • Basic Wiki, also for Web Apps (thought I had this on GitHub as well, but I guess not; will have to dig it up)
